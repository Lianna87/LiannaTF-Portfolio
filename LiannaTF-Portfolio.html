about me

Hey, there! I'm a Bristol-based web-developer with a robust background in linguistics and a history of English language teaching. I am currently transitioning into the realm of computer programming, finding intriguing connections between these seemingly dissimilar disciplines.

My expertise in linguistics has equipped me with a keen ability for identifying patterns, deciphering syntax structures, and understanding intricate systems - skills that I've found to be remarkably applicable in the world of web-development. Both fields demand a deep understanding of logic and syntactic structures, as well as a creativity for effective communication of complex ideas.

The shift from language study to web development has been a considerable challenge, but the analytical thinking, problem-solving skills and attention to detail honed in linguistics have proven invaluable in writing clean, efficient code and debugging with a linguistic detective's eye.

I'm excited to continue this journey, merging my love for language with the ever-evolving world of technology. Whether it's crafting compelling narratives or constructing innovative web solutions, I'm on a quest to create meaningful connections in this digital landscape through the artistry of words and code. By merging the precision of coding with the nuances of language, I aim to engineer compelling digital experiences that resonate with users.
